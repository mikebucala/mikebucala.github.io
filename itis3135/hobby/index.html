Lacrosse

What is Lacrosse ?

Lacrosse is a sport that is very similar to hockey in a lot of ways. Two teams of 10 players play a total of 4 quarters. The goal of the game is to score points by throwing the lacrosse ball into the opposing teams net. Each team has 1 goalie, 3 defenders, 3 mid-fielders, and 3 attackers. The game starts with a face off. A face off is when two opposing players line up in front of each other at center field and when the referee blows the whistle both players try to get the ball simultaneously. Now lets talk some about the rules of lacrosse.

The rules of lacrosse are very similar to hockeys rules. Every player can be penalized and placed in the penalty box for a varying amount of time (depending on the severity of the penalty) leaving their team down a man. Here are a list of a couple of different penalties and what causes them:

  • Personal Fouls:
    • Slashing: When a player violently swings their stick and strikes a player
    • Illegal Cross-Check: When a player makes contact with another player and their hands are spread apart
  • Technical Fouls:
    • Offsides: Offsides occurs when there are too many defensive or offensive players on one side of the field
    • Holding: Holding is when a player keeps a player from moving in any way using their hands, stick, etc.

About me

I played lacrosse in highschool for 3 years. Throughout my three years I played as a defenseman for my highschool. I was by no means a prodigy at lacrosse but I wasn't terrible at it. I mainly just played to have fun with my friends throughout highschool but we fell in love with the game and wanted to be good. After a lot of hard work and pushing through medical issues, I wasn't half bad at the end of my high school lacrosse "carrer". I enjoyed learning a new sport and competing together on a team with my friends. I learned not only how to play the game of lacrosse but important life skills too such has: no matter how many times you're knocked over, you have to get back up and keep fighting.

My Best Time's in Lacrosse:

One of the best times I got to experience while playing lacrosse at my high school was beating one of our rivals in our last game of my senior year. My senior year was cut short due to COVID-19 and this game happened the day before my school went completely online. We thought that we were only going to be online for about two weeks and it was going to be a little vacation for us, we had no idea that after that day our lives and many others would be changed forever. After a long back and forth game, we came out with the win. At least for many of the seniors in my class look back on that day fondly.

My Highschool Team: Southern Alamance Patriots

My Highschool team was the Southern Alamance High School Patriots. We were a decent sized school, but not a lot of people played lacrosse in my hometown. My team had a couple of people who grew up playing and helped a lot of the newer guys learn a lot the basics a lot faster. My sophmore and most of my junior year, we lost almost every game we played not because they were a lot better than us, but most of our players were new and would make simple mistakes. With a little time we got pretty good but it never mattered to us if we were winning or losing because no matter what after every single game we could learn something to become better.
