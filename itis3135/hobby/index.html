Lacrosse

What is Lacrosse ?

Lacrosse is a sport that is very similar to hockey in a lot of ways. Two teams of 10 players play a total of 4 quarters. The goal of the game is to score points by throwing the lacrosse ball into the opposing teams net. Each team has 1 goalie, 3 defenders, 3 mid-fielders, and 3 attackers. The game starts with a face off. A face off is when two opposing players line up in front of each other at center field and when the referee blows the whistle both players try to get the ball simultaneously. Now lets talk some about the rules of lacrosse.

The rules of lacrosse are very similar to hockeys rules. Every player can be penalized and placed in the penalty box for a varying amount of time (depending on the severity of the penalty) leaving their team down a man. Here are a list of a couple of different penalties and what causes them:

  • Personal Fouls:
    • Slashing: When a player violently swings their stick and strikes a player
    • Illegal Cross-Check: When a player makes contact with another player and their hands are spread apart
  • Technical Fouls:
    • Offsides: Offsides occurs when there are too many defensive or offensive players on one side of the field
    • Holding: Holding is when a player keeps a player from moving in any way using their hands, stick, etc.

How lacrosse began

Lacrosse was created and first played by Native Americans. Lacrosse began as a game know to them as stickball. The original game of lacrosse was played over huge stretches of open fields that were sometimes miles long. There was no "out of bounds" and the only rule was not to tough the ball with your hands.The game would start by someone throwing the ball into the air (instead of a faceoff) and someone catching it or getting it off the ground. Lacrosse was eventually shown to french missionaries who spread lacrosse to other regions of the world.

Why did the Native Americans start playing lacrosse

The Native Americans started playing lacrosse as a way to train and "harden" warriors within their tribe. They would play lacrosse to settle disputes between tribes, or even just for recreation or religious resasons. The way Native Americans played lacrosse back then was a lot different from the way it is played now. There were no rules beside using your stick instead of your hands. This led to a much more extreme and violent version of lacrosse and was the reason it was used to train warriors.

Some of the religious reasons they would play lacrosse for are:

  • Giving honor/respect to the heavens
  • Honoring the dead/fallen
  • Apart of a funeral service
